Types of Foreclosures

Discover the different foreclosure processes and how they impact your ability to recover lost equity.

Tax Deed Foreclosures

If a property owner does not pay their real estate taxes, the county will foreclose on the property. Any equity you may have accumulated while you owned the property is still yours minus the payment for the real estate taxes and any liens that were filed against you or the property. This type of foreclosure is called a tax deed foreclosure.

Mortgage Foreclosures

If a property owner does not make their monthly mortgage payment, eventually the lender will foreclose on the property. Once foreclosed on, the equity in the property still belongs to the previous owner. After the foreclosure, there may be the surplus funds that are due you. Any liens or real estate taxes should be paid before the amount of surplus is determined. This type of foreclosure is called a judicial foreclosure.

Unclaimed State Funds

If you left any money in a bank account and forgot to close the account, after a certain period of time the State where the bank is located will retrieve these funds and place them in the state‚Äôs unclaimed monies account. This money is waiting for you to claim it. But, if after a period of time, if you have not claimed the money, it is given to the state.
